# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We pledge that everyone participating in the Django Toastify community will be treated with respect and consideration, regardless of their background or identity.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- Being respectful and inclusive of different opinions and experiences
- Providing constructive feedback and criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy toward other community members
- Welcoming newcomers and helping them get started

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, discrimination, or hostile behavior toward individuals
- Offensive language or slurs
- Unwelcome sexual advances or attention
- Threats, intimidation, or violent language
- Doxxing or sharing personal information without consent
- Other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Reporting Issues

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it to the project maintainers at [contact@example.com] or open a confidential issue. All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly.

## Enforcement

Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in:

- A warning or request to cease the behavior
- Temporary suspension from community participation
- Permanent removal from the community

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all community spaces, including:

- GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions)
- Code and documentation
- Any official communication channels

This Code of Conduct is adapted from the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/), version 2.0, available at https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2_0/code_of_conduct.html.
